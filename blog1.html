Importance of Unified Laws for Marriage and Divorce

  • C A MAJEED
  • 2 Feb 2019
  • 2 min read
  • 2

Marriage, a culturally recognised union between a man and a woman is an important part of family life. The right to marry and have a family is considered one of the basic fundamental rights to life under the laws of the Indian Constitution. A legally and socially sanctioned marriage is regulated by laws, rules, customs and beliefs. These marriage laws govern the manner of entering into marriage, lay down the age condition and also ensure the rights of the partners concerning each other.

Religion Specific Marriage Laws in India

India being the most religiously and ethnically diverse country, Indian marriage law seems to be diverse and religion-specific. They are governed by a system of personal laws based on religion, caste, faith, and beliefs. These laws are codified after due consideration of customs and religious texts and largely focused on marriage and divorce, custody and guardianship, inheritance etc.

Hindu personal law is codified under the Hindu Marriage Act, Hindu Succession Act, Hindu Minority and Guardianship Act, and Hindu Adoptions and Maintenance Act while Zoroastrians are subject to the Parsi Marriage and Divorce Act.

Muslim personal law is based on their religious texts and acts such as the Shariat Application Act and Dissolution of Muslim Marriages Act.

Christian marriages and divorces come under the Indian Christian Marriages Act and the Indian Divorce Act.

Furthermore, some laws disregard religion altogether and they are known as a Special Marriage Act, under which Inter-religion marriages take place.

Additionally, to safeguard distinct regional identities, there are certain exceptions for the states of Assam, Nagaland, Mizoram, Andhra Pradesh and Goa concerning family law under the Indian Constitution.

Shift Towards Unified Laws for Marriage and Divorce

The above-mentioned marriage rules are religion-specific marriage laws reflecting the age-old religious beliefs and there is no stability in how issues like marriage, divorce, succession and adoption are treated for people belonging to different communities. In addition, these laws also give preference to customs and even now certain evil customs are prevalent in our country.

These religion-specific marriage laws include certain provisions that are biased towards men. This clashes with Article 14 of the Constitution, which guarantees equality before the law. To fulfil the constitutional guarantee of gender equality, the laws are modified now, to give women equal rights in marital relationships as well as in property matters. But still, ample reforms need to be done. The religious marriage laws also pose problems for couples belonging to different castes or religions despite having a special marriage act.

All the above-mentioned scenarios lead to the need for a Uniform Civil Code that provides equal rights to all citizens irrespective of the community they belong to.

Uniform Civil Code (UCC) - What it is and why does it matter?

A uniform civil code calls for the formulation of one law, applicable to all citizens of India with regards to personal matters such as marriage, divorce, custody, adoption and inheritance.

UCC comes under Article 44 of the Constitution, which lays down that the state shall aim to put forth a Uniform Civil Code for the citizens all over the territory of India.

The issue has been at the centre of political discussion and debate for over a century.

When enacted the code will replace the system of disintegrated personal laws codified based on religious beliefs and the same civil law will then apply to all citizens irrespective of their faith. UCC aims to provide gender equality and provide protection to religious minorities, while also promoting nationalism through unity.

The Way Forward

The varied marriage laws we practice in India is due to the diverse culture and religion we practice, yet it is often a cause of confusion and paves way for serious legal problems. The way ahead is to achieve the constitutional goal of equal rights by enacting uniform civil laws which would ensure common laws related to marriage, divorce, inheritance, custody etc for all religions while also respecting the unity in diversity of our country.
