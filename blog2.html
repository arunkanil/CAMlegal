Surnames mother's or father's - any illegality?

  • C A MAJEED
  • 2 Feb 2019
  • 2 min read
  • 2

Surname is commonly referred to as the last name and it acts as the primary signifier of one’s identity. The practice of adopting surnames varies by culture. For some reason, it is universally accepted that the surname for children of married parents is usually inherited from the father. Traditionally, it was the custom for a woman to take up her father’s surname before marriage and post marriage, she is expected to take her husband’s surname. In recent years, some women prefer to retain their old surname while few adopt a new surname. However, even today, the idea of having a mother's name as a surname is not fairly accepted by society and there's a feeling that the person in question is estranged from the father.

Every child has the right to use his mother's or fathers or both names as a surname. If the child is happy with the choice of their surname, no one has the right to order that only the father's name is to be used in the surname.

And also, there's a growing trend of young parents to include both of their names in their child name. It is the choice of parents to give their child either the maternal surname or paternal surname or both. Parents have to make sure that the same name has been written on all the documents. Therefore, it is legally possible and acceptable for a person to adopt a maternal surname or paternal surname or both.

Can a surname be changed?

One can either change, add or remove a surname from their existing names.

The requirement is an affidavit in the stamped paper and the Affidavit must be signed by a First Class Judicial Magistrate or Notary Public. This affidavit is an important legal document for all legal formalities and filings in the future.

After obtaining the name change affidavit, it is necessary to publish an advertisement in Gazette,1 Local Language daily and English Daily.
