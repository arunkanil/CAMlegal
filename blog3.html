Free speech can be in anonymity?

  • C A MAJEED
  • 2 Feb 2019
  • 2 min read
  • 2

The emergence of social media facilitates a global ecosystem of social interaction where one can express their views freely, publicly on social issues. Even though social media paves way for self-expression, many people don't want the things they express online to be connected with their real-time identities. People choose to speak anonymously as they are concerned about political or economic consequences, harassment, or even threats to their lives.

Right to Anonymity in India

Anonymous communications play an important role in our political and social discourse.

The Information Technology Act, 2000 and The Indian Penal Code, 1860, Article 19 (1) (a) deals with fundamental freedom of speech and expression and Article 21 fundamental right to life and personal liberty including the right to privacy, deals with the right of anonymity.

However, no such law on protecting anonymity has been enacted so far. Neither the legislature nor judiciary has particularly guaranteed such rights in any judgement.

Grounds of Restrictions

It is important to maintain the appropriate balance between protecting anonymity and enforcing consequences for the abusive behaviour that arises due to anonymity.

India has remarkably protected the right to freedom of speech and expression “in the interest of” the public by imposing “reasonable restrictions” for the maintenance of social order.

  • Anything that incites to or motivates to commit heinous crimes that would undermine the security of the State.
  • Anything that disturbs public tranquillity or public peace.
  • The right to speech should not affect the morality of society adversely.
  • Freedom of speech doesn't allow defamation of any type.
  • Freedom of speech and expression does not grant a right to incite people to commit offence.
  • It doesn't allow anyone to challenge sovereignty or to express something which will result in a threat to the integrity of the country.

Bottom Line

Cyber anonymity along with free speech creates a sense of security and an open environment to discuss the political and social discourse of one community or nation. With them came the hundreds of thousands of anonymous trolls and malicious anonymous users breeding anti-social behaviour creating a negative impact on personal lives and also has emerged as a threat to the nation. Hence, there is an urgent need for stronger laws that protect anonymity and at the same time enforce negative consequences that arise due to anonymity.
